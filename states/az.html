Arizona — State Legislature 2026

ARIZONA 2026: CAMPAIGN STRATEGY BRIEF

Flipping Both Chambers of the Arizona State Legislature

Prepared: March 14, 2026 For: Arizona Democratic Legislative Campaign Committee / Individual Candidates

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Arizona Democrats need +3 Senate seats and +4 House seats (net) to flip both chambers and create a Democratic trifecta with Governor Hobbs. The current GOP advantage is 17-13 in the Senate and 33-27 in the House. Seven battleground legislative districts have been identified by the DLCC and ADLCC as primary targets: LD2, LD4, LD9, LD13, LD16, LD17, and LD23.

The environment is favorable: - Trump approval in Arizona sits at -11% to -15% disapproval margin - Democrats have flipped 9 state legislative seats nationally in special elections under Trump; Republicans have flipped zero - Prop 139 (abortion rights) won 61.6% statewide in 2024 -- carrying every legislative district - The $1 billion ESA voucher program is deeply unpopular (65% voted against expansion in 2018) and a 2026 ballot initiative to reform it will drive education voters to the polls - The DLCC has committed $50 million nationally with Arizona as a top "flip" target, plus a dedicated $2 million Arizona investment - Hobbs' "An Arizona We Can Afford" message polls well on affordability, housing, and schools

The unified campaign message: "An Arizona We Can Afford" -- affordability, public schools, healthcare access.

Arizona Independent Redistricting Commission

  • Current maps are stable. Arizona's independent commission draws maps after each decennial census; mid-decade redistricting is constitutionally barred absent a court order.
  • GOP legislature has proposed expanding the commission from 5 to 9 members and tightening population deviation requirements. This would go to voters in November 2026 but cannot affect 2026 races.
  • No active litigation threatens current district boundaries.

BALLOT MEASURE SYNERGY: ESA VOUCHER REFORM

The "Protect Education Act" ballot initiative (filed March 13, 2026 by AEA and Save Our Schools Arizona) will be a massive downballot driver: - Caps ESA eligibility at $150,000 household income (special needs exempted) - Requires fingerprint clearances for voucher-funded school employees - Bans luxury item purchases (currently: diamond rings, lingerie, gaming laptops have been purchased with ESA funds) - Returns unused ESA funds to public schools - Requires academic accountability for ESA students

The ESA program has ballooned from 15,000 students to 100,000+ and from $175M to over $1 billion annually. It drains an average of $300,000 from every public school in Arizona.

This initiative needs 255,000+ signatures by July 2. If it qualifies, it will drive education-minded voters to the polls in November -- the exact suburban voters Democrats need in LD2, LD4, LD9, and LD13.

Meanwhile, the GOP legislature is retaliating by moving to defund the teachers' union (AEA) -- creating an "us vs. them" narrative that benefits Democrats.

THE TALARICO FRAMEWORK: FAITH-FORWARD MESSAGING FOR ARIZONA

What Talarico Did in Texas

James Talarico won the March 2026 Texas Democratic Senate primary by defeating Rep. Jasmine Crockett with a progressive Christian faith-forward message. His core framework: - "Politics is not left versus right, but top versus bottom" - Christian faith as the basis for caring for the poor, healthcare for all, good public schools, welcoming the stranger - Direct challenge to Christian nationalism: "I'm not running against Christianity -- I'm running for the Christianity I was raised in" - Performed strongly in border counties with Catholic Tejano voters

Application to Arizona's LDS and Evangelical Populations

Arizona's religious landscape: 27% evangelical Protestant, 6% LDS/Mormon, 21% Catholic, 24% unaffiliated (Pew).

For LDS voters (LD13, LD16 especially): - LDS theology emphasizes: agency (free will), community service, caring for the poor, welcoming missionaries and immigrants, family stability, fiscal prudence - Frame: "My values come from the same place yours do -- from a belief that we're called to serve our neighbors, protect our children's education, and build communities where families can thrive." - Never attack the church. Frame Republican extremism as departing from shared values. - LDS voters who voted for Harris cited Jan. 6, election denialism, and anti-immigrant rhetoric as dealbreakers -- lean into these

For evangelical voters (LD2, LD4, LD17): - Talarico's message resonates: "Loving your neighbor isn't a liberal idea. It's a commandment." - Frame education funding as stewardship: "What does it say about our priorities when we fund diamond rings with voucher money but can't pay our teachers?" - Healthcare access as moral imperative

For Catholic/Latino voters (LD16, LD9): - Catholic social teaching aligns with Democratic economic message - Immigration: "This isn't about politics. It's about whether we treat families with dignity." - Talarico's border county success is directly transferable

Key tactical note: This framework does NOT require candidates to be personally devout. A Talarico campaign volunteer said he liked the faith-based message despite not being "really" faithful himself -- it signaled integrity and values.

CROSS-CUTTING ISSUES AND MESSAGING

1. Education and ESA Vouchers

The single most powerful issue across all six target districts. - $1 billion ESA program drains ~$300K from every public school - Luxury item purchases (diamonds, lingerie, gaming laptops) with taxpayer voucher money - 65% of voters rejected ESA expansion in 2018; Republicans expanded it anyway in 2022 - 2026 "Protect Education Act" ballot initiative will drive turnout - GOP is retaliating by trying to defund the teachers' union

Message: "They're taking $1 billion from your kids' classrooms and spending it on diamond rings and gaming laptops. When teachers organized to stop it, they tried to shut the teachers down. That's not accountability -- that's corruption."

2. Reproductive Rights

  • Prop 139 passed with 61.6% -- won EVERY legislative district
  • GOP lawmakers are now pushing "preborn child" legislation to undermine Prop 139
  • Multiple Republican incumbents voted for the 15-week ban or against repealing the 1864 ban

Message: "You voted to protect your rights. They voted to take them away. Who's really listening to you?"

3. Housing Affordability

  • Median home price in Maricopa: $474,000 (up 65% since 2019)
  • Only 39% of Arizona workers at median wage can afford a one-bedroom apartment
  • Democratic proposal: 60-day "shock clock" giving families first access before corporate investors; repeal state preemption of local rent control

Message: "A home shouldn't cost more than a family can earn. But while corporations buy up our neighborhoods, the legislature protects the corporations."

4. Water

  • Phoenix area faces a 4.9 million acre-foot groundwater deficit over the next century
  • Developers are building in areas where officials found insufficient water supply
  • Pinal County agriculture facing Colorado River cuts
  • Rio Verde Foothills (near LD2) had water literally shut off

Message: "You can't drink a developer's promise. We need leaders who protect our water, not their donors."

5. Immigration (Defensive Positioning)

Arizona GOP has introduced record immigration bills in 2026: criminalizing "unlawful alerting," requiring immigration status verification for public benefits, etc.

DO NOT lead with immigration. It's a losing offensive issue. Instead: - Defend: "I believe in secure borders AND treating families with dignity. My opponent wants to make it a crime to tell your neighbor the police are coming." - Redirect: "While they were writing immigration bills, they weren't fixing your water, your schools, or your rent. That's a choice -- and it's the wrong one."

CLOSING ASSESSMENT

Arizona 2026 is the Democrats' best opportunity to flip a state legislature since 2018. The fundamentals are aligned:

  1. Trump backlash is real and measurable in Arizona suburbs
  2. Prop 139 proved that abortion rights carry every legislative district
  3. The ESA ballot initiative will drive education voters to the polls
  4. Republican overreach on vouchers, abortion undermining, and anti-union retaliation creates openings
  5. LDS voter disillusionment with MAGA is a structural shift, not a blip
  6. Latino voter growth provides a demographic tailwind if registration and turnout are invested in
  7. Kevin Volk's LD17 win is proof that Republican-leaning districts can be flipped with the right candidate and message

The biggest risks are: - Underfunded southern Arizona campaigns (LD16, LD17) - Candidate recruitment gaps (LD2 Senate, LD13 House, LD16 House) - Third-party spoilers (Green Party in LD13) - Immigration as a Republican wedge if Democrats don't control the narrative - Hobbs drag if her reelection becomes a liability rather than a turnout driver

Bottom line: The seats are there. The environment is there. The question is whether the investment, candidates, and infrastructure match the opportunity. This is not a cycle to be cautious. This is the cycle to go all in.
