TIER 1: WISCONSIN SENATE (2 SEATS — THE PRIZE)

Current: R 18 - D 15. Need 17 for majority = net +2.

Target Districts: - SD-21 (Racine): Van Wanggaard (R). Pure toss-up (0-2 pt margin). Challenger: Trevor Jung (transit director, former city councilman). - SD-5 (Brookfield): Rob Hutton (R). Leans D by 5-10 pts. Challenger: Rep. Robyn Vining (state assemblywoman). - SD-17 (Spring Green): Howard Marklein (R). Challenger: Kelly Peterson (former journalist, teacher). - SD-31: Jeff Smith (D) — MUST DEFEND.

All four districts: Both Baldwin and Harris won majorities in 2024.

Funding: Senate Dem Committee raised $771,870 in H2 2025 non-election year — 2x Republican counterpart, highest-ever.

Maps: Act 94 (Feb 2024) replaced 12-year gerrymander. Graded "A." Dems flipped 14 seats in 2024 under new maps.

TIER 1: MINNESOTA HOUSE (TIED 67-67)

One seat decides control. 21 of 134 contests decided by <10% in 2024. DFL won two Jan 2026 specials to restore tie. Every district matters.

Senate (34-33 DFL): Top pickup: SD-35 (Anoka) — Jim Abeler (R) won by 186 votes in 2022. DFL recruiting Angela Nelson (elementary school principal).

TIER 1: MICHIGAN HOUSE (4 SEATS)

Current: R 58 - D 52. Need 56 = net +4. Three races decided by <1.5% in 2024. Fair maps from independent commission. Sabato: Toss-up.

Senate defense: D 19 - R 18. May 2026 special in SD-35 could tie it. DLCC invested $250K.

TIER 2: OHIO — BREAK SUPERMAJORITY

Not a flip. But ~7 House seat gains deny GOP supermajority. Combined with Acton (D) winning governorship = veto power restored. Real play is 2030 redistricting.

TIER 2: IOWA/KANSAS — HOLD GOVERNORSHIPS

Both have open seats (Reynolds/Kelly term-limited). Losing these eliminates last Dem checks in those states.
