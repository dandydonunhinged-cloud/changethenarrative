PENNSYLVANIA STATE SENATE 2026: CAMPAIGN STRATEGY BRIEF

Prepared for the PA Senate Democratic Campaign Committee

Date: March 14, 2026

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

Objective: Flip the Pennsylvania State Senate from Republican (27-23) to Democratic control. Democrats need 3 net seats for outright majority (26-24) or 2 net seats for a 25-25 tie with Lt. Gov. Austin Davis breaking ties. This would create PA's first Democratic trifecta since 1993.

Current Landscape: - Governor Shapiro at 60% approval, rated Solid Democrat for reelection -- enormous coattail potential - 2025 municipal elections produced a statewide "blue wave" -- Democrats swept Bucks County row offices, Supreme Court retention, school boards - Trump's "One Big Beautiful Bill" cuts $51-53B in Medicaid from PA over 10 years, threatens 310,000 Pennsylvanians' coverage - DOGE layoffs creating economic anxiety across suburban Philadelphia - Democratic voter registration advantage narrowed to ~171K statewide, BUT suburban engagement surging

The Math: - OFFENSE: Must flip 3 of 4 targets (SD-6, SD-16, SD-24, SD-40) - DEFENSE: Must hold SD-36 (Malone, Lancaster -- won special by 530 votes in Trump+15 district) - If SD-36 falls: Must win ALL 4 offensive targets. There is no margin for error.

Recommendation: Prioritize SD-6 (Bucks) and SD-24 (Montgomery) as Tier 1 pickups, SD-16 (Lehigh/Bucks) as Tier 2, SD-40 (Monroe/Lackawanna/Wayne) as Tier 3 reach. SD-36 defense is existential.

MACRO ENVIRONMENT

Josh Shapiro Coattail Effect

Shapiro's 60% approval (Quinnipiac, October 2025) is the highest since polling began in June 2023. Cook Political Report shifted the PA Governor's race to Solid Democrat. His opponent, State Treasurer Stacy Garrity, trails significantly. Shapiro has a massive cash-on-hand advantage.

Strategic implications: - Shapiro's team plans to invest heavily in winning Senate control for a trifecta - His coattails helped down-ballot Democrats in 2025 municipal sweep - Coordinate Senate candidate messaging and appearances with Shapiro's reelection tour - Joint events in Bucks, Montgomery, and Lehigh counties maximize turnout in target districts - Shapiro won SD-36 territory by <1% in 2022 -- even modest coattail effect helps Malone defense

Voter Registration Trends

The statewide Democratic registration advantage has eroded from 630,000 (March 2021) to ~171,000 (December 2025). Between October 2024 and October 2025, Democrats lost 158,000 registrants while Republicans lost only 30,000. Independents/third-party voters now represent 12.7% of registrations and growing.

However, registration does not equal votes. The 2025 municipal elections demonstrated that Democratic turnout infrastructure in the collar counties is far superior to what registration numbers suggest. In Bucks County, despite Republicans achieving a registration advantage, Democrats swept every row office with nearly 50% turnout in an off-year election.

Strategic implication: Invest in persuasion of unaffiliated/independent voters AND turnout operations rather than obsessing over registration gap.

Federal Headwinds for Republicans

The Trump administration's "One Big Beautiful Bill" is a gift to Democratic messaging: - Medicaid: 310,000 Pennsylvanians set to lose coverage. Work requirements take effect December 31, 2026 -- right after the election. $51-53 billion in federal Medicaid funding cuts over 10 years. - SNAP: PA would face $650 million in additional costs to maintain food assistance. Work exemption age raised from 54 to 64. - DOGE: Pennsylvania lost 700 federal workers in a single month (Jan-Feb 2025). NIH funding cuts threaten Philadelphia-area medical institutions. - Mental health funding: State budget cuts to county mental health services at a time of rising demand.

These cuts disproportionately impact suburban families, seniors, and working-class communities in every target district.

SCENARIO ANALYSIS

Scenario A: Win 3 offense + hold SD-36 = 26-24 Democratic majority Best case. Full legislative control. Requires sweeping SD-6, SD-24, and one of SD-16 or SD-40 while holding SD-36.

Scenario B: Win 2 offense + hold SD-36 = 25-25 tie, Lt. Gov. Davis breaks ties Most realistic path. Win SD-6 and SD-24 (both Tier 1) while holding SD-36. Democrats set the agenda but need Republican votes to pass bills without the tiebreaker.

Scenario C: Win 4 offense + lose SD-36 = 26-24 Democratic majority Near-impossible. Requires sweeping all four targets including the Tier 3 reach in SD-40. This scenario underscores why SD-36 defense is non-negotiable.

Scenario D: Win 2 offense + lose SD-36 = 24-26 Republican majority Net +1 only. Republicans retain control. This is the nightmare scenario if resources are spread too thin.

Recommended target: Scenario B with maximum effort toward Scenario A.

CLOSING ASSESSMENT

The 2026 PA Senate map is the most favorable for Democrats in a generation. The confluence of: - A popular governor with enormous coattails - Trump administration policies that directly harm suburban families - 2025 municipal election momentum proving Democratic organizational strength - Republican incumbents with anti-choice voting records in pro-choice districts - A narrowing but still-positive statewide registration advantage - Rising independent voters who trend Democratic in suburban races

...creates a genuine window for a trifecta. The last one was 1993. The next opportunity of this caliber may not come for another decade.

The risk is overconfidence and resource dilution. The SD-36 defense is the foundation. Without Malone, the math becomes nearly impossible. Build from the foundation up: Hold SD-36, flip SD-6 and SD-24, compete hard in SD-16, and invest in SD-40 if the wave builds.

The wind is at Democrats' backs. But wind doesn't vote. Organization does.
