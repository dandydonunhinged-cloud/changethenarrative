WISCONSIN STATE SENATE FLIP — Campaign Strategy Brief

2 seats to flip. 1 to defend. Democratic trifecta if governor wins.

Compiled by Specter — 2026-03-14

THE MATH: SD-5 is the lock. SD-21 is the linchpin. SD-17 is insurance. SD-31 is the firewall.

Win any 2 of SD-5/SD-21/SD-17 = majority (17-16), even if SD-31 falls.

SD-5 (BROOKFIELD/WAUWATOSA) — BEST PICKUP. OPEN SEAT.

Rob Hutton (R) NOT RUNNING. Harris +6. Crawford (Supreme Court) won bigger here.

Robyn Vining (D): State Assemblywoman, photographer, small business owner, former pastor, theology degree from Trinity Evangelical Divinity School, military family. $114K cash.

Mike Roberts (R): Physical therapist, no legislative experience, entered after Hutton retired. Placeholder.

Messaging: Faith + small business + military family. "Career politician" impossible to stick. Hutton's record on blocking postpartum Medicaid and defunding schools becomes Roberts' inheritance.

Target: D+7. Lock this first.

SD-21 (RACINE) — TOSS-UP. THE LINCHPIN.

Van Wanggaard (R): Former cop. 100% Right to Life. NRA life member. FOXCONN CHAMPION — voted for the $3B deal that delivered 768 jobs instead of 13,000. Two of three buildings sit empty. Residents displaced. $1.2B in taxpayer infrastructure. "Racine is still really, really angry."

Trevor Jung (D): Adopted from Russian orphanage at age 2. Youngest Racine alderperson since 1862. Transit director ($10M budget). $133K raised. Platform: "The Five Ts" — Talent, Training, Transportation, Technology, Tourism.

The kill shot: Foxconn. Local, visceral, Wanggaard's vote. "They handed $3 billion to Foxconn and got empty buildings. I manage real public budgets and deliver real results."

Attack conversion: "Born in Russia" → "His story IS the American story. Attacking where someone was born is what politicians do when they can't defend their own record."

Target: D+2-3. Every vote matters. Harris +1.2.

SD-17 (SPRING GREEN) — STRETCH TARGET. FARM COUNTRY.

Howard Marklein (R): Co-chair, Joint Finance Committee. He literally writes the state budget. Sits on $4B surplus while rural schools hold referendums, farmers go bankrupt, wells are contaminated.

Jenna Jacobson (D): State Assemblywoman, grew up rural Polk County, Frederic High School. Agriculture Committee member. $115K raised. Rural roots + Dane County suburban base.

Also in primary: Corrine Hendrickson — ran child care business 18 years, closed it because funding failed her kids. $3K cash. Most powerful personal story in Wisconsin.

The kill shot: Farm bankruptcies up 46%. Trump tariffs crushing soybean farmers. Marklein has the budget pen and chose not to help. "The man who writes the state budget sits on a surplus while your farm goes under."

241 school referendums in 2024. Communities taxing THEMSELVES because Marklein's committee won't fund schools.

Target: D+2-3. Harris +1, Baldwin +4.7. Ticket-splitters exist here.

SD-31 (EAU CLAIRE) — DEFEND.

Jeff Smith (D incumbent): Assistant Senate Minority Leader. $150K raised — doubles opponent.

Jesse James (R): Senator from DIFFERENT DISTRICT who moved to run here. Carpetbagger.

The kill shot: "I've lived and served here for decades. My opponent moved here from another district."

Target: D+3-5.

CROSS-CUTTING WEAPONS

Tariffs

Farm bankruptcies up 46%. Average household lost $1,300-$1,700 purchasing power. Soybean farmers lost China market. Farmer support for tariffs dropped from 70% to 51% in 6 months. Works in ALL four districts.

School Funding

241 referendums in 2024. Communities voting to raise their OWN property taxes because Rs won't fund schools. Republicans sit on $4B surplus. "You shouldn't have to raise your own taxes to fund your kids' schools."

Reproductive Rights

1849 abortion ban still on the books. Wanggaard 100% Right to Life. Drove suburban women in 2024, will again.

Crawford Baseline

Supreme Court race April 2025: D won 55-45 statewide, won ALL FOUR battleground districts by more than statewide margin. With governor's race driving higher November turnout, floor is higher.
